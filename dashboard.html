The TPM + Claude + Jira MCP workflow

1
Jira stays the source of truth
The assumption: tickets are well-written and up to date. Claude reads directly from your live board via the Jira MCP connector — not a spreadsheet export, not a screenshot. Real data, real ticket keys, real timestamps.
2
Claude compresses the bookkeeping
Board scan → sprint schedule → risk register → dependency map → status report → Agile specs → trade-off matrices. Work that takes a TPM 4–6 hours manually takes minutes. Claude drafts. The TPM reads and decides.
3
Human validation is the non-negotiable gate
Every comment posted to Jira was reviewed by the TPM before it went live. Claude drafts; the TPM decides what's accurate, what needs editing, and what gets posted. The AI accelerates — the human owns the output.
4
The time saved goes to actual program thinking
Catching the GDPR violation in UNIFY-11 wasn't a Claude catch — it was a TPM catch enabled by having time to read the whole board instead of writing status updates. That's the real value: reclaiming thinking time.
